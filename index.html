The Harsh Reality

For many families, food, water, and survival come before education. When money is tight, boys are prioritized, and girls are forced to leave school to work or care for their families. 55% of women in low and middle-income families never get the chance to complete their education.

Safety Fears

Parents hesitate to send their daughters to school, fearing for their safety. In conflict-affected areas, girls are 2.5 times more likely to drop out. Lack of secure transportation and unsafe routes prevent millions from attending school.

Women’s Economic Empowerment

Women’s economic empowerment is often overlooked. For too long, women have been undervalued & underrepresented in the economic world. Now is the time to change that. By recognizing their value & contributions, we can build a more just and equal society.

Today, women are leading companies and transforming their communities. Homemakers have become entrepreneurs. They are going above & beyond to establish a life for themselves. Your support can help them achieve their dreams.
